abstract = """This study integrates an agent-based model of human decisions with a large-scale, process-based distributed hydrological model to examine food–energy–water trade-offs under climate change. Applied to the Columbia River Basin with a Decision Scaling analysis, the framework reproduces historical irrigation-water consumption and streamflow while representing human decisions within the hydrologic cycle. Results indicate that trade-offs among irrigation, hydropower, and streamflow intensify under hotter and wetter conditions, with region-specific temperature thresholds. The findings can inform long-term basin management and Columbia River Treaty discussions."""
