abstract = """This study examines bidirectional feedbacks in coupled natural–human systems by linking RiverWare with agent-based models of irrigation decisions in Washington's Yakima River Basin. The coupled framework better reproduces historical irrigation diversions and streamflow than the baseline river-system model. The analysis also explores how farmers' social norms influence decisions and how a water-reallocation policy changes diversion behavior. The results illustrate how coupled models can support environmental policymaking while highlighting the need to evaluate model structure and parameter uncertainty."""
